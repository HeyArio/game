# Product

## Register

brand+product

## Users

Intellectually curious adults who want a fast daily mental rep — forecasters, analysts, tech-adjacent readers, and anyone who wants to sharpen their judgment under uncertainty. They arrive via share links or word-of-mouth, spend ~2 minutes on the daily case, and return because the streak is worth protecting. On the brand surface (landing page) they are evaluating whether Quorum is worth their time. On the product surface (Play / Leagues / Quests / Profile) they are mid-ritual, focused on the case.

## Product Purpose

Quorum is a free daily browser game at quorumdaily.com. Four frontier AI models answer the same genuinely debatable question each day; an independent judge model (Arbi) picks the most defensible answer; users earn XP for matching the judge, predicting the crowd, and keeping a daily streak. Success looks like: a player opens Quorum daily without prompting, the streak becomes a social identity, and the game earns a reputation as a credible way to train judgment — not just a curiosity.

## Brand Personality

Bold · Precise · Curious

Voice is confident and direct — a publication that plays games, not a game that aspires to be a publication. It takes the question seriously even when it is playful. It doesn't over-explain; it trusts the player.

## Anti-references

- **Not Duolingo**: Quorum shares a green palette, a mascot, and gamification, but should not read as a Duolingo clone. Avoid the over-saturated friendly-green-everything aesthetic; let the question and reasoning carry the page.
- **Not a news/media site**: No editorial typography, article-width columns as the primary layout, or publication-style navigation. This is a game, not a feed.
- **Not generic SaaS**: No hero-metric stat cards ("10,000 players"), no feature grids, no "streamline your workflow" copy. The product is the daily case, not a platform.
- **Not a quiz app**: No Kahoot-style bright CORRECT/WRONG flash patterns, no trivia recall aesthetic. Quorum rewards reasoning, not recall — the design should reflect that distinction.

## Design Principles

1. **The case is the hero.** Every screen defers to the question and the arguments. UI chrome — nav, XP bars, streak counters — supports without competing.
2. **Judgment, not trivia.** Visual language should signal deliberation: scale over speed, precision over flash. The game feels more like a courtroom than a quiz show.
3. **Daily ritual discipline.** Fast, clean, repeatable. No decorative complexity that adds friction to the 2-minute loop. Every screen should feel completable.
4. **Bold confidence, not noise.** One committed design choice beats three hedging ones. The palette, typography, and layout should make decisive moves — Wordle-confident, not SaaS-cautious.
5. **Practice what you preach.** If Quorum rewards clear reasoning, the interface should reason clearly. Copy is specific; layout communicates hierarchy; nothing is there by default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline: 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text, keyboard navigation, and screen reader semantics. Reduced motion should degrade gracefully; no content should be gated behind a CSS transition.
